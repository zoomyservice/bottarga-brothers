The Madar Family

A Story That Begins
in Safi, Morocco

Brothers Herbert and Jean Madar didn't set out to build a business. They set out to share something extraordinary they'd loved their whole lives.

"Bottarga isn't just one of many products — it's all we do."

— Herbert & Jean Madar, Bottarga Brothers
The Beginning

Roger Madar &
The Comosa Factory

The story begins with Roger Madar — father of Herbert and Jean — who owned Comosa, a sardine fishing and canning company on the Atlantic coast of Safi, Morocco in the 1950s.

Roger was a man of the sea and of tradition. Among the catches that came through his factory, Grey Mullet roe held a special place. He knew how to cure it the old way — salt, patience, and time.

When the Madar family emigrated to Canada in the mid-1960s, Roger brought the craft with him.

The Family Story

From Morocco
to Montreal
to Your Table

The Madar family's journey spans three generations, two continents, and one singular passion — the finest cured mullet roe in the world.

Discover Their Selection
1950s — Safi, Morocco

Roger Madar & Comosa

Roger owned Comosa, a sardine fishing and canning operation on the Atlantic coast. He became an expert in curing Grey Mullet roe — a skill passed down through generations of Mediterranean fishing communities.

Mid-1960s — Montreal, Canada

The Family Arrives

The Madar family immigrated to Montreal. Roger began sourcing fresh Grey Mullet from Waldman's fish market on St. Laurent, and continued crafting bottarga at home using the same traditional method he'd brought from Morocco.

Every Friday Night

A Ritual is Born

Bottarga became a weekly Friday night tradition in the Madar home — sliced thin, served as an aperitif before dinner, paired with Arak or a glass of Scotch. Herbert and Jean grew up with this taste. It became part of who they are.

The Mission

Bottarga Brothers is Founded

Herbert and Jean Madar set out to bring fantastic bottarga from around the globe to existing enthusiasts — and to introduce North American consumers to this ancient culinary delight. They source only from world-class producers. They carry only one product. They do it right.
