Classic Italian

Pasta Bottarga

One of the most popular Italian dishes featuring bottarga. A briny, oceanic pasta that comes together in minutes with barely any ingredients. Recipe courtesy of Herbert Madar.

Serves 1
Time 15 min
Bottarga Sardinian Gold
Ingredients
  • 3 oz spaghetti
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • Handful of fresh arugula (optional)
  • 2 tbsp grated Sardinian Gold Bottarga
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Method
  1. Cook pasta to al dente. Rinse with cold water and set aside.
  2. In a medium frying pan on medium-high heat, fry minced garlic in hot olive oil briefly — before it browns. Add arugula and cook until wilted.
  3. Add the cooked spaghetti to the pan. Toss gently until the pasta is hot and completely coated in the olive oil and garlic.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to a plate.
  5. Sprinkle a generous amount of grated Sardinian Gold Bottarga over the top. Serve immediately.
Aperitif

Bottarga Toast

Can be served as an aperitif or just a tasty snack. Also very simple and fast to make — three ingredients, zero effort, maximum impression.

Serves 2–4
Time 5 min
Bottarga Boutargue Impériale
Ingredients
  • French baguette or ciabatta
  • Butter
  • Grated Boutargue Impériale
Method
  1. Cut bread into slices approximately ¾ inch thick.
  2. Toast lightly and allow to cool completely — never butter warm toast.
  3. Apply a light layer of butter to the cooled toast.
  4. Sprinkle grated bottarga generously on top. That's it. Enjoy.

Pair with Arak, a dry sparkling wine, or prosecco. Serve at room temperature for the fullest flavor.

Mediterranean

Couscous Bottarga

An easy Mediterranean recipe made in heaven. Very easy to make and so delicious — a perfect weeknight dish that feels like something special.

Serves 1
Time 15 min
Bottarga Grated Gold
Ingredients
  • 1 cup prepared couscous (cooked)
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • ¼ cup green onion
  • 2 tbsp grated bottarga
  • Sea salt & freshly ground black pepper
Method
  1. Prepare couscous according to package instructions. Set aside.
  2. In a pan, warm olive oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic until fragrant. Add green onion and cook briefly.
  3. Fold in the cooked couscous. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Plate and finish with a generous sprinkle of grated bottarga over the top.
Breakfast

Sunny Side Bottarga

The simplest luxury breakfast imaginable. The richness of a perfectly fried egg meets the briny depth of grated bottarga — extraordinary in under five minutes.

Serves 1
Time 5 min
Bottarga Any variety
Ingredients
  • 1–2 fresh eggs
  • 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • Grated bottarga, to taste
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Fresh toast or crusty bread, to serve
Method
  1. Heat butter or olive oil in a small pan over medium-low heat.
  2. Crack eggs into the pan. Cook gently until whites are just set and yolks remain runny.
  3. Slide onto a plate. Immediately grate bottarga generously over the top.
  4. Season with a pinch of sea salt. Serve with toast.

Try with the Greek Avgotaraho for a more delicate flavor, or Sardinian Gold for something bolder.

Pro Tips

Getting the Most
From Your Bottarga

Use bottarga like a fine cheese — at room temperature, sliced thin with a sharp non-serrated knife. Or grate it like a truffle over anything hot. The heat opens up the flavor.

✦

Always bring bottarga to room temperature before serving

✦

Grate directly over hot food — the heat blooms the flavor

✦

Less is more when you first start — the flavor is intense

✦

Try it with Arak, dry white wine, or prosecco as an aperitif

✦

Re-wrap tightly after opening; store refrigerated at all times
