Certified & Authenticated

Kosher Bottarga

Three of our bottargas carry full kosher certification — authenticated by the Rabbinical Office of Rome and the Grand-Rabbinat de Paris. Among the very few in the world.

Why This Matters

Bottarga is a cured fish roe — a product that requires particular attention to sourcing, salting, and processing to meet kosher standards. Bottarga Brothers worked directly with certifying rabbinical authorities in Rome and Paris to ensure full compliance. Our certified products are also Kosher for Passover — a distinction that goes beyond standard kosher certification and makes them suitable when most other fish products are unavailable.

Certified Products

Each certification is product-specific and independently verified by a rabbinical authority in the country of origin.

Standard Kosher

Kosher Certified

Confirms the product meets all year-round kosher dietary laws — sourced from kosher species, processed under rabbinical supervision, free of non-kosher ingredients, and with no cross-contamination.

Sardinian Gold carries this certification.

A Higher Standard

Kosher for Passover — Pareve

A more stringent certification ensuring the product has never come into contact with leavened grain (chametz). Pareve means it contains neither meat nor dairy — suitable alongside both. This allows it to be served during Passover when most other fish products cannot be used.

Boutargue Classique & Boutargue Impériale carry this certification from the Grand-Rabbinat de Paris.
